    Due to his profiency in vyākaraṇa, he was called Pratyagra-Patañjali, i.e. 'The Modern Patañjali', by his contemporaries (The Indian Antiquary vol. 43, pg. 129 T. S. Kuppuswami Sastri).
    Raghavan in his intro to Śāhendravilāsa (pg. 41) suggests a sojourn at Madura for Rāmabhadra Dīkṣita on account of the mention of Madura as the venue of the enactment of of his Śṛṅgāratilakabhāṇa. See also Thiruvengadathan pg. 11
    Ṛgvedin, Kauṇḍinyagotra (Raghavan intro to Śāhendravilāsa pg. 40)
    Recipient of 4 shares of Thiruvisanallur, as he was a master in 4 śāstras: alaṁkāra, vyākaraṇa, mīmāṁsā, and vedānta (see Thiruvengadathan and Raghavan)
    He was hailed as pratyagrapatañjali, dvitīyaprācetasa, dvitīyavālmīki, sāhiytaratnakośa, śabdabrahmasvarūpī, and rāmabhaktyabdhi by his contemporaries (see Thiruvengadathan pg. 13)
